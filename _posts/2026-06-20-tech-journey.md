As my technical skills grew, I wanted to move beyond tutorials and personal projects to solve real-world problems with data. This drive led me into professional roles where I could apply analytics, data governance, and decision-support systems to complex operational environments.

### Grounding Theory in Reality

My first industry experience was as a Data Scientist at Advantage Health Africa. There, I worked directly with operational healthcare datasets, handling data cleaning, validation, and exploratory analyses while supporting data-driven healthcare product development. This role exposed me to the unique realities of messy, live data and reinforced my interest in building technology systems that streamline industry workflows.

Today, I work as a Clinical Data Scientist at Medbury Pharmaceuticals, sitting at the intersection of business operations, data management, and strategic decision-making. In this role, I build analytics models, dimensional data structures, and reporting assets that drive organizational efficiency across inventory analytics, financial reconciliation, and master data management.

### Turning Raw Data into Revenue Assurance

A significant part of my day-to-day responsibility involves transforming raw operational records into actionable insights. I conduct cost-of-goods and consumption analyses to identify usage trends, uncover billing discrepancies, and protect revenue. By reconciling financial records directly with inventory movements and consumption patterns, I help ensure data accuracy across multiple business functions. 

Additionally, I focus heavily on product and operational analytics. Through inventory velocity analysis and product master data management, I support core decisions related to inventory planning, pricing, vendor negotiations, and product performance tracking.

### The Human Side of Data Governance

Beyond the numbers, I have become deeply involved in data governance and quality management. I help design and maintain data protection processes, access-control procedures, audit documentation, and operational standards that safeguard our data integrity. Working closely with different business units has taught me that effective data systems rely just as much on trust, consistency, and accountability as they do on technology.

One of the most rewarding aspects of this work is collaborating with stakeholders across finance, procurement, warehousing, human resources, and operations. These interactions have sharpened my ability to translate technical findings into practical business recommendations, using data as a functional bridge between different teams.

### Connecting the Dots

Looking back, every stage of my journey has built upon the last:
*   **Pharmacy** taught me to think scientifically and systematically.
*   **Software engineering** taught me how to architect and build.
*   **Data analytics** taught me how to uncover hidden insights.
*   **Data science** introduced me to intelligent, automated systems.

My professional experience has shown me how these disciplines come together to solve practical problems that affect people, organizations, and healthcare outcomes. The path has not been linear, but it has been consistently driven by a desire to learn, solve problems, and create solutions that make a meaningful impact.
