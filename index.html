About

From the pharmacy bench to production data systems

A Bachelor of Pharmacy gave me a working fluency in the language of healthcare — clinical terms, supply chains, regulation, and the cost of getting data wrong. I now bring that domain knowledge to the engineering side of the problem.

My work focuses on stable data infrastructure: designing efficient ETL pipelines, orchestrating data flows across relational and NoSQL stores, and modelling raw operational data into governed, analytics-ready warehouses.

Beyond the infrastructure, I translate processed data into business intelligence that technical teams and non-technical stakeholders can act on — closing the loop between raw collection and a decision someone actually makes.
